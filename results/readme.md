Observations:
The robot consistently detected fire with about 90% accuracy, responding in under three seconds.
It navigated obstacles smoothly without collisions, showing reliable control. Upon detection, the pump activated instantly, and the servo nozzle swept across the flame zone. 
The battery backup lasted 30–40 minutes during continuous operation.

Performance Metrics:
Performance tests showed strong results: fire detection accuracy reached 90%, ensuring dependable identification of flames. The robot’s response time was less than three seconds, minimizing fire spread risk. Its battery backup of 30–40 minutes supported extended demonstrations.
These metrics highlight efficiency, responsiveness, and practicality for educational firefighting experiments.

Testing Environment:
Experiments were conducted in an indoor lab setup with controlled flame sources such as candles and alcohol burners. Randomly placed obstacles tested navigation and collision avoidance. The environment simulated realistic conditions while ensuring safety. This setup allowed accurate measurement of detection, response, and suppression performance under repeatable, small‑scale scenarios.

Conclusion:
The robot demonstrated reliable performance in controlled environments, effectively reducing human intervention in fire detection and suppression. Its compact, low‑cost design makes it suitable for educational use and robotics demonstrations. Results confirm its potential as a prototype for teaching automation, safety engineering, and embedded systems in practical, hands‑on contexts.
